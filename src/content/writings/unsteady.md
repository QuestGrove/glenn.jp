Before I knew what ADHD was — before I had the word — I just thought I was broken in a specific way that other people weren't.

Not dramatically broken. Quietly broken. The kind of broken where you look fine from the outside and feel like you are failing a test everyone else got the instructions for.

## The texture of it

It feels like trying to hold water in your hands. Not the water spilling — that comes later. Just the constant effort of cupping your hands tighter, concentrating harder, trying not to lose what you're carrying before you get where you're going.

By the time you arrive somewhere, you're exhausted from the holding. And nobody saw you working that hard, because it's invisible work.

## What changes when you have a word for it

The word doesn't fix anything. But it changes your relationship to the thing.

Before the word: *I am someone who cannot focus.*
After the word: *I have a brain that focuses differently under different conditions.*

The second sentence opens a door. The first one seals you in a room.

This is why the ADHD manual exists here. Not to explain the condition from above, but to leave notes for the next person who is still in the sealed room and doesn't yet know there's a door.
